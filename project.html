The Project

Restoring the woodland.

The aim is simple: remove invasive species, restore habitat, and make space for a wider mix of plants, birds, bats, insects, and fungi.

Approach

What we are doing

We are clearing invasive growth where it is damaging the woodland, improving access for management work, and encouraging natural regeneration.

Goal

More habitat, more variety

The project is about building a healthier mix of habitats: trees, understorey, deadwood, edges, open patches, and wet areas where possible.

1

Remove

Take out invasive species and reduce pressure on native growth.

2

Restore

Improve the woodland structure and bring neglected areas back into better condition.

3

Monitor

Track wildlife and habitat changes so the work can be adjusted over time.
